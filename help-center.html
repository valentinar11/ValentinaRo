Managing Your Profile

How to update your company information and documents
To become a UNOPS supplier, you must first register on the United Nations Global Marketplace (UNGM) at www.ungm.org. Complete your company profile with all required information, including business registration documents, financial statements, and capability statements. Once your UNGM registration is approved, you can access the UNOPS Procurement Platform using your UNGM credentials.
Required documents typically include: Certificate of Incorporation or Business Registration, Tax Registration Certificate, audited financial statements for the past 2-3 years, company profile/capability statement, references from previous clients, and any relevant certifications or licenses for your industry. Specific requirements may vary by tender.
Use the search and filter options in the Opportunities section to narrow down tenders by organization, type (RFQ, RFP, ITB), country, and keywords. You can also set up email alerts on UNGM to receive notifications when new opportunities matching your profile are published.
Response timelines vary depending on the complexity of the procurement. Request for Quotations (RFQs) typically allow 2-4 weeks, while Request for Proposals (RFPs) and Invitations to Bid (ITBs) may allow 4-8 weeks. Always check the specific deadline and time zone indicated in each opportunity. Late submissions are not accepted.
Yes, you can submit offers for as many opportunities as you wish, provided you meet the eligibility criteria for each. However, ensure you have the capacity to fulfill all contracts if awarded. Each submission should be tailored to the specific requirements of that tender.
Evaluation criteria depend on the procurement type. Generally, offers are assessed on technical compliance, price competitiveness, supplier qualifications, and past performance. For RFPs, technical evaluation typically weighs 60-70% and price 30-40%. The specific criteria and weightings are detailed in each tender document.
